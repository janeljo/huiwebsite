Program Benefits

  • Workshops on study skills, college survival, and academic programs
  • Sense of community before entering UH
  • Math Workshop
  • Writing Workshop
  • Tutors & Mentors
  • Summer student fees paid for
  • Free Summer school books
  • One Free 3 college credit course from the list below
  1. Educational Foundations 310 (Education in American Society):
    Interrelated historical, philosophical, and socio-cultural contexts of education with an emphasis on identifying, analyzing, and deliberating on contemporary ethical issues, problems, and applications. DS

  2. English 100 (Composition I):
    Introduction to the rhetorical, conceptual and stylistic demands for writing at the university level; instruction in composing process, search strategies, and writing from sources. FW

  3. Ethnic Studies 101 (Introduction to Ethnic Studies):
    Basic concepts and theories for analyzing dynamics of ethnic group experiences, particularly those represented in Hawai'i, and their relation to colonization, immigration, problems of identity, racism, and social class. DS

  4. Pacific Studies 108 (Pacific Worlds: An Introduction to Pacific Islands Studies):
    Introduces students to the geography, societies, histories, cultures, contemporary issues, and arts of Oceania, including Hawai'i. Combines lectures and discussion emphasizing Pacific Islander perspectives and experiences. DS

Eligibility

Interested students MUST be a high school senior graduating 2016 - 2017 school year AND be accepted to the University of Hawaiʻi at Manoa. Students must also meet at least ONE of the following requirements:

  • Will graduate from a Hawaiʻi Title I School
  • Pell-Grant Eligible
  • A member of an underrepresented community at the University of Hawaiʻi at Manoa

Peer Mentors

Students will be grouped with peer mentors who support and assist them in their transition. Each peer mentor session focuses around a topic and students will engage in various activities surrounding that topic. Students will discuss transitional topics to ease their anxieties and their transition prior to the start of their first day. In addition students will debrief about each topic and have the opportunity to put these topics into action. These peer mentors will typically be in the same field/interest as the student and will be there to guide them through the summer and academic school year.
